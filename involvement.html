Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers

The Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers, SHPE for short, is the largest association in the United States for Hispanics in STEM. It has chapters nationwide, all with one mission: empower Hispanics to each their fullest potential through volunteering, mentorship and workshops, amongst other events.

My first semester in college, I was not able to be as active as I would've loved to be. However, in my second semester I have been as active as I could. I have been attending any workshop, general meetings, and socials. I also attended the RDLC Leadersip Convention in March. I am also in the process of joining the Technical Affairs Committee. I hope to be more involved in the coming year.

POSSE Foundation Scholarship

POSSE is not a club, but a scholarship. POSSE is found in major cities across the US. Each city has different partner schools, Texas A&M is a partner school for Houston and Atlanta. Only four students from each school in each city can be nominated. These students then have to undergo three separate rounds of interviews. The first and last interview are group interviews, while the second is a solo. In the group interviews, the students are carefully watched, rated on their ability to support, work and lead others. By the third interview, there is only about 25 students left, and only 10 are chosen. These 10 students then become each others support system throughout college and are awarded full tuition scholarships. These students are also expected to attend weekly meetings, workshops and check ins with their mentor. POSSE students are expected to thrive and become leaders in their respective community.
